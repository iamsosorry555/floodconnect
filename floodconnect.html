The Problem

  • Many people do not know when or where floods will happen, causing individuals to get trapped.
  • Rescue teams take longer to navigate and reach high-risk areas effectively.
  • Severe property damage and lives are lost due to a lack of easy-to-use localized warnings.
  • Vulnerable communities cannot clear out or secure assets in time.

Our Hypothesis

If people had access to accurate, real-time information about flood-affected areas, then they would be able to plan, avoid dangerous zones, and reduce the risk of losing their lives, homes, and crops.

This will also help emergency services respond more quickly and efficiently, making communities safer during flood events.

🚨 Emergency Alert System

Enables individuals to instantly fire off distress telemetry including exact GPS coordinates directly to dispatchers and rescue teams the moment safety conditions fail.

💬 Direct Authority Communication

A built-in dedicated connection line allowing live chat between civilian users and nearby rescue squads to streamline emergency instructions and coordinate rescues.

🤖 AI-Powered Risk Assessment

Aggregates precipitation arrays, ascending river level sensors, and historical vectors to dynamically display who is secure, who is trapped, and where assets should be deployed next.

📊 Advanced Forecasting & Research

Employs predictive AI models to calculate the impending statistical likelihood of flash floods, providing small micro-communities structural time to prepare before disaster triggers.

The Engineering Behind The System

Flood Connect addresses a huge gap in civic safety tech by condensing intricate enterprise early-warning models into an accessible, youth-friendly mobile dynamic interface.

1. Runoff & Inflow Tracking

To accurately capture river behavior, the architecture maps how much incoming rain transforms into moving river volume through:

Rain Gauges Runoff Computer Models Tributary Flow Sensors

The models continuously calculate soil absorption levels, vegetation retention, and geometric terrain gradients to estimate water movement.

2. River Level & Velocity Monitoring

Real-time physical danger is classified through continuous real-time field hardware streams measuring both height and displacement:

Ultrasonic Sensors Radar Level Metrics Doppler Flow Meters Pressure Level Loggers

Combining surface height tracking with fluid speed variables allows the AI to determine the volumetric hazard of rising currents.

3. Radar-Rain Gauge Merging Architecture

While rain gauges provide precise localized ground truths, weather radars provide spatial broadness. Flood Connect's back-end combines these using mathematically integrated spatial techniques:

Mean Field Bias Adjustment Kriging with External Drift (KED) Bayesian Data Integration

Hardware design concepts feature ruggedized, waterproof sensor nodes configured along river banks that sync in sequence via short-range network links (e.g., peer-to-peer Bluetooth mesh topology) to maintain field integrity and pipe high-resolution inputs to our central app engine.
